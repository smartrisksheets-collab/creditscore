Scoring engine

Rigorous. Quantitative. Built for Nigerian capital markets.

Every assessment is evaluated across five dimensions of financial health, following internationally recognised credit analysis best practice — calibrated for the Nigerian operating environment.

Liquidity analysis
Measures the issuer's ability to meet short-term obligations from liquid assets. A critical signal for instruments with tenors of 270 days or less.
Short-term coverage
Profitability & growth
Evaluates earnings quality, margin sustainability, revenue growth trajectory, and return on assets — determining whether the business generates sufficient profit to support its obligations.
Earnings quality
Leverage & capital structure
Assesses total debt burden relative to assets and capital base. Identifies over-leveraged issuers whose debt capacity may be exhausted before maturity.
Debt position
Debt service & coverage
Scores the issuer's capacity to service debt from operating earnings, including interest coverage and cash flow adequacy — the most direct measure of repayment risk.
Repayment capacity
Financial distress prediction
Applies a globally recognised multi-factor quantitative model that combines five balance sheet and income statement variables to generate a composite score predictive of corporate financial distress. Widely used in fixed income credit analysis worldwide.
Bankruptcy signal
